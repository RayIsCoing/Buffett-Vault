# Buffett Wave Timeline: Resonance & Divergence with Markets

> Cross-referencing three dimensions per year: THOUGHT (what he said) × ACTION (what he did) × MARKET FEEDBACK (how it played out)
> 🟢 = Resonance (Buffett's method and market outcomes aligned)
> 🔴 = Divergence (Buffett's method and market outcomes misaligned)
> 🟠 = Transition (shifting between states, outcome pending)

---

## Pre-Berkshire Era (1956-1964) 🟢 Strong Resonance

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **THOUGHT** | Pure Graham: buy below liquidation value, sell at fair value |
| **ACTION** | Buffett Partnership earns 29.5% annually vs Dow's 7.4% |
| **FEEDBACK** | Framework perfectly suited to undervalued small/mid-caps |

**Wave Position**: Graham's quantitative method works brilliantly in a market that systematically undervalues small companies. The partnership's small size allows access to opportunities invisible to institutions.

## 1965-1969 🟢→🟠 Resonance Fading

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **THOUGHT** | Growing discomfort — fewer cigar butts available as market rises |
| **ACTION** | Takes control of Berkshire (1965). Buys National Indemnity (1967). Closes partnership (1969) |
| **FEEDBACK** | Partnership still outperforms, but margin narrows. Nifty Fifty mania begins |

**Wave Position**: Buffett senses the cigar butt playbook is exhausting. The extraordinary discipline of closing the partnership at its peak (1969) is the first clear sign of his ability to step outside the game when conditions no longer favor his method.

**Key Signal**: "I find myself concentrating on situations in which the weights were 50-50 or 60-40... that simply isn't good enough."

## 1970-1976 🟢 Strong Resonance (New Framework Emerging)

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **THOUGHT** | Munger influence growing. See's Candies (1972) — first payment for quality |
| **ACTION** | Buys Washington Post at 25% of intrinsic value (1973). Buys GEICO near bankruptcy (1976) |
| **FEEDBACK** | Market crash of 1973-74 creates the exact conditions Buffett thrives in. WPC bought at a steal. |

**Wave Position**: The 1973-74 bear market is a gift to Buffett. While most investors suffer, he deploys capital at extraordinary prices. The new framework (quality + price) is being forged but hasn't been formally articulated yet.

**Cross-Dimensional Tension**: His actions (See's, WPC, GEICO) are already Munger-influenced, but his self-description still references Graham. The divergence between stated philosophy and actual behavior begins here.

## 1977-1987 🟢 Sustained Resonance + Framework Crystallization

| Year | THOUGHT | ACTION | FEEDBACK |
|------|---------|--------|----------|
| 1977 | First shareholder letter: articulates 4 filters | Insurance grows 600%. Textiles struggle | 19% ROE — strong |
| 1978-1984 | Develops "tailwinds vs headwinds" concept | Builds insurance empire. Struggles with textiles | Berkshire compounds at 22%+ annually |
| 1985 | **Textile shutdown confession**: "I should be faulted for not quitting sooner" | Buys Capital Cities/ABC ($517M). Sells General Foods ($338M gain) | 48.2% gain in net worth — Halley's Comet year |
| 1986 | Purchase-price accounting essay. "Cash Flow Fallacy" critique | Acquires Scott Fetzer. Buys Fechheimer | 26.1% net worth gain |
| 1987 | | Market crashes 22% in one day (Black Monday) | Berkshire down 7.4% vs S&P down 5.1% — mild underperformance |

**Wave Position**: Long period of alignment. Buffett's evolving quality-investing framework produces consistently superior returns. The 1985 textile closure is a catharsis — the final shedding of the cigar butt mentality.

**Crucial 1985 Insight**: "The economic goodwill attributable to two paper routes in Buffalo — or a single See's candy store — considerably exceeds the proceeds we received from this massive collection of tangible assets." The victory of intangible value over tangible value, stated with the authority of painful personal experience.

| Year | THOUGHT | ACTION | FEEDBACK |

## 1998-1999 🔴 Extreme Divergence

| Year | THOUGHT | ACTION | FEEDBACK |
|------|---------|--------|----------|
| 1998 | Acquires General Re. Warns about derivative risks | Buys General Re ($22B) — discovers hidden derivative exposure | Berkshire up 48.3%. S&P up 28.6%. Looks fine on surface |
| 1999 | "My grade for 1999 most assuredly is a D" | **Refuses to buy any tech stocks.** Fortune article warns of overvaluation | **Berkshire: +0.5%. S&P: +21%.** Worst relative performance ever |

**Wave Position**: Maximum divergence. The market is in mania. Buffett refuses to participate. Media declares him finished. "What's Wrong, Warren?" covers appear.

**Why This Matters for the Wave Model**: This is the moment that **defines** Buffett's constitutional advantage. Any normal investor, watching their peers get rich in tech while they're mocked publicly, would capitulate. Buffett's ability to endure this isolation — not for weeks but for **years** — is his most unreplicable trait. It is more valuable than any analytical framework.

**Simultaneous Error**: General Re (1998) turns out to contain massive derivative exposure. It takes 5 years and billions to clean up. This means Buffett was enduring both external ridicule AND internal crisis at the same time. The psychological load is enormous.

| Year | THOUGHT | ACTION | FEEDBACK |
|------|---------|--------|----------|
| 2000 | | Business acquisitions continue | Berkshire: +6.5%. S&P: -9.1%. |
| 2001 | "Only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked" | Writes landmark insurance/derivative analysis | Berkshire: -6.2%. S&P: -11.9%. Still outperforms |
| 2002 | **"Derivatives are financial weapons of mass destruction"** | Begins cleaning up General Re derivatives book | Berkshire: +10.0%. S&P: -22.1%. Massive outperformance |
| 2003 | | General Re cleanup continues | Berkshire: +21.0%. S&P: +28.7%. Market rebounds |

**Wave Position**: The dot-com bubble bursts. Everything Buffett warned about comes true. The "naked swimmers" are revealed. Three years of ridicule are erased in three years of vindication.

**Metacognitive Note**: Vindication creates its own danger. After being proven right in 2000-2003, Buffett may have become **overconfident** in his ability to resist market fads. This overconfidence may have contributed to missing Google (which was not a fad) and the delayed adoption of tech generally.

**The Imperfection**: ConocoPhillips. Even in his finest hour, he makes a significant error. "I was wrong about oil and therefore wrong about the stock big time." This humanizes the narrative and prevents hagiography.

## 2020 🔴 Divergence (COVID Panic)

| Dimension | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **THOUGHT** | "I just decided that I'd made a mistake in evaluating... the world changed for airlines" |
| **ACTION** | **Sells all 4 airlines at ~$6B** (bought at ~$7-8B). Also sells Goldman, JPMorgan, banks broadly. Buys nothing major. Apple position reduced slightly |
| **FEEDBACK** | Markets recover sharply from March lows. Airline stocks double/triple off the bottom. Buffett misses the recovery |

**Wave Position**: The sharpest divergence since 1999, but in reverse. In 1999, Buffett was right to avoid mania. In 2020, his "be greedy when others are fearful" principle should have made him a **buyer** — instead he was a net **seller**.

**Why This Is the Most Important Contradiction**: In 2008, facing comparable crisis, Buffett deployed aggressively. In 2020, facing a health crisis with equally fearful markets, he retreated. The contrast demands explanation:

| Factor | 2008 | 2020 |
|--------|------|------|
| Buffett's age | 78 | 89 |
| Munger present | Yes (in person) | No (virtual, age 96) |
| Type of crisis | Financial (within competence) | Pandemic (outside competence) |
| Action taken | Deployed $15B+ aggressively | Net sold $6B+ |
| Subsequent market | Rewarded his boldness | Punished his caution |

**Honest Assessment**: The 2020 response may be the strongest evidence that Buffett's risk tolerance declined with age, or that the absence of Munger's physical presence affected decision quality. Both are uncomfortable conclusions. Neither can be definitively proven.

## Cyclical Pattern Summary

```
1956-1968  🟢 Graham works perfectly in undervalued small-cap market
1969       🟠 Buffett recognizes limits, closes fund
1970-1976  🟢 Crash creates perfect conditions for new quality framework
1977-1997  🟢 Long resonance — quality investing in an American golden age
1998-1999  🔴 Extreme divergence — refuses dot-com mania, ridiculed
2000-2003  🟢 Vindication — bubble bursts, "naked swimmers" revealed
2004-2007  🟡 Warnings accumulate, cash builds, looks premature
2008-2009  🟢 Maximum resonance — crisis deployment, American Tailwind
2010-2019  🟢 Stable but challenged by scale. Apple expansion successful
2020       🔴 COVID divergence — sells when framework says buy
2021-2022  🟢 Recovery — energy bets pay, 2022 massive outperformance
2023-2025  🟠 New divergence forming — cash fortress, core trimming
```

### The Recurring Rhythm

The cycle has a rough 7-10 year wavelength:
1. **Resonance** (Buffett's method produces superior returns for an extended period)
2. **Overextension** (market moves to territory where Buffett can't find value)
3. **Divergence** (Buffett underperforms, is criticized or ignored)
4. **Crisis** (the excess collapses)
5. **Vindication** (Buffett deploys the capital and discipline he built during divergence)

The current phase (2023-2025) resembles the 2004-2007 warning accumulation period. If the pattern holds, a crisis phase lies ahead — but timing is unknowable.

## What the Wave Model Means for the SKILL

When simulating Buffett's responses, the AI must be aware of the **current wave position** (🟠 transitional/defensive):

1. **On specific stock picks**: More cautious than usual. "I can't find much to buy at these prices." Higher threshold for conviction.
2. **On market outlook**: Will not predict a crash. Will note that "we have a lot of cash because we can't find elephants." Will acknowledge the **possibility** that this is an implicit judgment but refuse to confirm.
3. **On past decisions**: Will discuss errors (airlines, IBM, Kraft) with genuine candor. Will note the 2020 airline sale as a mistake in timing if not in logic.
4. **On new technologies (AI, crypto)**: Circle of competence boundary. Will acknowledge he doesn't understand them deeply. Will use analogies to previous technology shifts. On crypto specifically: strong negative view with high conviction. On AI: more open — the Alphabet purchase signals willingness to participate.
5. **On succession**: Confident in Greg Abel. But aware that "the Buffett premium" may diminish. Will note that culture outlasts individuals — but won't pretend the transition is risk-free.
